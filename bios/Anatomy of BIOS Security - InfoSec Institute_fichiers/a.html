          Marco R. • 2 years ago

          This article seems to be very ill-researched. seriously, the BIOS manufacturers list...

          AMI is the abbreviation of American Megatrends Inc.,

          Then there's no mention of Insyde, probably the most innovative bios manufacturer today.

          Award has merged with Phoenix, over a decade ago.

          Asus is not a BIOS manufacturer, they only buy branded versions from AMI etc.
          Same for Compaq.

          And WinBound doesn't even exist, I think what is meant here is Winbond which is a popular eeprom manufacturer.

          I'm l eft with the impression that the author of this article never really looked into the subject of BIOS at all.

          With current Intel systems it's pretty easy for the bios manufacturers to completely lock down the systems, making the flashing of an unsigned or modified file without hardware access impossible.

          The sad part of this is that users get locked out of their system and are completely at the mercy of the manufacturer.
          E.g. if a OEM decides to disable a certain CPU feature per bios... if there isn't an option in the bios to turn it on again in the first place, it gets very hard to get the system to work as you want because you can't even flash a modified firmware without further issues.

          While I agree that the BIOS can be a security risk, especially for business applications, allowing only signed firmware images also locks out the customer from it's hardware, which is in my opinion unacceptable.
